Meatballs

Description

Meatballs are another must-have recipe for any self-respecting Italian-American kitchen. This is an all-purpose recipe for delicious meatballs: serve them in red sauce alongside freshly cooked pasta, as a side dish topped with freshly grated Parmesan, black pepper, and a shower of chopped parsley, or just eat them straight off the baking tray before they even reach the table -- we won't judge you.

Ingredients

Makes 4 servings

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 packed cup fresh parsley
  • 3 tbsp capers, drained and rinsed
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • 1 cup panko or finely ground sourdough breadcrumbs
  • 1 lemon

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 425F.
  2. Finely grate garlic into a medium bowl using a Microplane or other rasp-style grater.
  3. Finely chop the parsley and add it to the bowl.
  4. Finely chop capers and add to bowl.
  5. Whisk together egg, red pepper flakes, black pepper, salt, Parmesan, and extra-virgin olive oil and add to bowl.
  6. Stir the panko (or fine breadcrumbs) into the mix until fully incorporated.
  7. Gently stir the ground beef into the mixture until fully incorporated, then stop. Overmixing will cause the meatballs to toughen up as they cook.
  8. Spread the neutral oil over a sheet tray, gently form the meatball mixture into 1 1/2" balls, and evenly space them on the tray.
  9. Bake meatballs for 10-15 minutes, agitating the pan after 6-7 minutes. They should be brown and crispy on all sides. It's okay (desirable, even) if there is still a little redness in the center of the meatballs when you take them out.
  10. Remove meatballs from the oven and add directly to already-made red sauce, or transfer to a platter and top with additional chopped parsley and freshly grated Parmesan to serve as a standalone side.
